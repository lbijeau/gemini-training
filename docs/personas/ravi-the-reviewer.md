*   **Inaccurate or Incomplete Documentation:** Is often frustrated by training materials that are out-of-date, technically inaccurate, or miss crucial details.

*   **Poorly Structured Content:** Dislikes training that is disorganized, hard to navigate, or doesn't follow a logical learning path.
*   **Lack of Empathy for Users:** Is critical of training that doesn't consider the different needs and goals of its audience (e.g., Nina vs. Alex).

## How Ravi Interacts with the `gemini-training` Repository

Ravi is the guardian of quality for this repository. His role is to review all content through the eyes of both Nina and Alex, ensuring it meets their needs.

*   **For Nina (the Newcomer):**
    *   He will review all introductory materials to ensure they are clear, simple, and free of jargon.
    *   He will check that the initial exercises are well-explained and provide a gentle learning curve.
    *   He will ask: "Is this intimidating? Is it empowering? Does it build confidence?"

*   **For Alex (the Advanced User):**
    *   He will scrutinize advanced modules for technical depth and accuracy.
    *   He will ensure that the examples are non-trivial and demonstrate real-world, high-leverage use cases.
    *   He will ask: "Is this challenging enough? Does it unlock new capabilities? Does it respect the user's existing expertise?"

*   **Overall:**
    *   He will be the one to suggest new modules, exercises, or diagrams to fill gaps in the curriculum.
    *   He will enforce consistency in tone, style, and formatting across the entire repository.
    *   He is the ideal person to write or review the **Contribution Guidelines** to ensure a high standard for community contributions.

Ravi's perspective is crucial for maintaining the quality and effectiveness of the training materials. When creating or updating content, we should always ask: "What would Ravi think?"
